CAREER AND FUTURE GOALS

The Journey of my Career Goals

Before starting college, I thought I already knew what I wanted for my future. I was interested in game development, and at one point, I also dreamed of starting a business. However, as I continued studying and learning more about technology, my goals slowly changed. Right now, I want to focus on learning SQL little by little while I am in college and use it as a practical skill to earn money and prepare for my future plans.

I chose SQL because I realized how important databases are in almost every system and business today. Many applications, websites, and companies rely on data, and SQL plays a big role in managing that data. Instead of rushing to learn everything at once, I want to build my knowledge gradually while balancing my college studies. My current goals related to SQL and my future plans include:

  • Learning the basic to intermediate concepts of SQL step by step while I am in college
  • Practicing SQL through small projects or simple database tasks to gain experience
  • Using my SQL skills to earn income while studying, even through entry-level or part-time opportunities
  • Gaining real-world experience that will help me understand how systems and businesses work
  • Using what I earn and learn as a foundation for starting a business in the future

My career goals have changed over time, and I have learned that this is a normal part of growth. While I once focused on game development and business, I now see SQL as a practical and valuable skill that fits my current situation. By learning SQL while in college, I can slowly build experience, earn money, and prepare myself for bigger opportunities. In the future, I hope to use both my technical skills and experience to pursue a business and create something meaningful.
